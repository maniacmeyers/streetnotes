## A: AUDIENCE LAYER

**Target Audience:**
Mid-market AEs and SDRs running 6-10+ calls per day on Salesforce or HubSpot. Already using Gong, Fathom, or Fireflies for transcription but still manually updating the CRM. Spending 6-8 hours a week on admin instead of practicing their craft — storytelling, elevator pitches, customer conversations. The things that actually close deals.

**Underserved Angles:**
1. **"Admin is stealing your edge"** — Every sales tool talks about saving time. Nobody talks about what reps SHOULD be doing with that time. Storytelling practice, pitch refinement, real customer conversations. Jeff owns this angle because he's coached thousands of reps on exactly these skills.
2. **"Your transcription tool doesn't fix the problem"** — Gong and Fireflies record calls. Great. The CRM is still empty Monday morning. The gap between transcription and CRM data entry is where deals die. Nobody is talking about this gap.
3. **"Built by quota carriers, not engineers"** — Every competitor in this space was built by technical founders who studied the problem from the outside. Jeff and Michael lived it. 45 years combined, 11x President's Club. That credibility is unmatched.

**Your Differentiation:**
Two founders who lived the problem from both sides of the sales floor. Jeff carried a bag for 20 years and coached reps on the skills that actually close deals. Michael built and fixed sales teams for 20 years and knows what breaks when pipeline data is garbage. Neither is a tech founder who studied sales from the outside. The content doesn't need to manufacture credibility — it just needs to show up and be them.

**Content Themes:**
1. **"Time is your quota killer"** — The real cost of CRM busywork isn't hours lost. It's the storytelling practice, pitch refinement, and customer conversations that didn't happen. Admin doesn't just waste time — it steals the rep's competitive edge.
2. **Deal stories from the field** — Real scenarios from 45 combined years. Jeff's angle: the parking lot moments, the deals that died, the calls where one detail changed everything. Michael's angle: the forecast calls with garbage data, the teams he fixed by fixing their pipeline hygiene.
3. **Sales execution frameworks** — The 90-Second Debrief, Three Things Framework, storytelling structures, elevator pitch practice methods. Tactical content reps can use today. The actual revenue-generating activities StreetNotes frees them up to do.
4. **Building in public** — StreetNotes decisions, trade-offs, metrics, progress. Two founders, two perspectives. Jeff on the product and GTM. Michael on the market strategy and team adoption.
5. **Coaching and mindset** — Jeff's existing lane. The mindset shift from "I need to be disciplined about my CRM" to "I need to be disciplined about my craft." Michael's angle: leadership perspective on why he stopped policing CRM hygiene and started fixing the system instead.

**The thread running through all five:** StreetNotes handles the CRM so you can practice the things that actually make you money.

## COMPETITIVE SPEED STRATEGY

**The threat:** Larger players (Gong, Fathom, Fireflies, Clari) could build voice-to-CRM features once they see the concept gaining traction.

**The defense:** Speed to adoption + embedded workflows + testimonial moat + dual-founder brand.

1. **Get embedded fast.** Every team using StreetNotes inside their Salesforce/HubSpot is a switching cost. VBRICK is the first. Michael's network adds 3-5 more within 60 days.
2. **Collect testimonials aggressively.** Video testimonials from real sales managers is a moat no feature launch can replicate.
3. **Own the narrative with two voices.** Jeff owns the rep conversation. Michael owns the leader conversation. Two authentic founder brands covering both sides of the buying motion is nearly impossible to copy.
4. **Bottom-up adoption.** Individual reps adopt at $29/month. By the time the VP evaluates enterprise options, reps are already dependent on the workflow.
5. **Michael's network = speed.** 10 warm SMB introductions bypass the entire cold marketing funnel.
6. **VBRICK = proof.** A real Salesforce integration in a real sales environment with real usage data, before competitors even ship a feature.
