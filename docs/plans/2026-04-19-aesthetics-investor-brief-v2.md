## The Play

Build fast. Sell in 24–36 months. Get out before this becomes a feature in something bigger.

The AI-assisted field sales category is getting crowded in 12–18 months. We have a window now where nobody has claimed aesthetic sales reps as their customer. The companies who will eventually want this market — Veeva, PatientNow, Nextech, the Allergan tech arm, a PE roll-up — aren't building for it yet. We get in, prove ARR, and sell to one of them.

That's the thesis. It's not "build a $50M ARR SaaS empire." It's "build the only defensible rep-facing product in aesthetics, demonstrate traction, and be acquirable in 24 months."

The exit doesn't require 5,000 reps. It requires 500 reps and 3 brand-level accounts with their name on the contract.

## Why Brand Deals, Not Individual Reps

**Individual rep acquisition:** $199/month × 1 rep = $199 MRR. 100 reps = $19,900 MRR.

**Brand deal:** Revance licenses StreetNotes for their 150-rep US sales force at $179/seat = $26,850 MRR from one conversation. That's in the contract, billed quarterly, and it's what an acquirer reads as revenue.

Individual reps build the product story. Brand deals build the acquisition story. We need both, but brand deals are the engine.

**The target list for year 1 brand deals:**

| Company | US Rep Count | Why Now |
|---|---|---|
| **Revance** | ~200 | Aggressive challenger, still scaling, less IT bureaucracy than Allergan |
| **Evolus** | ~120 | Price-fighter positioning, their reps are at a disadvantage on intel |
| **Merz Aesthetics** | ~300 | Xeomin is losing ground to Daxxify, reps are under pressure |
| **InMode** | ~200 | Capital equipment, long cycles, reps desperately need pre-call prep |
| **BTL** | ~150 | EmSculpt Neo vs. CoolSculpting battle is all CI all the time |

Close 3 of these. That's 600+ seats at $179/seat = **$107,400 MRR / $1.3M ARR** from 3 deals. That's what a 24-month exit is built on.

## Trading Jeff's Time for Ad Spend

Jeff is stretched. Product, outreach, onboarding, content — one person can't do all of it. The math on cold outbound is brutal at scale: 100 LinkedIn messages → 8 replies → 2 demos → 0.5 customers. That's hours Jeff should be spending on product.

The fix: buy the conversations we'd otherwise spend hours initiating.

**LinkedIn Advertising for aesthetic sales reps:**
- LinkedIn allows exact targeting by employer + job title. We can reach every Revance, Evolus, Merz, InMode, and BTL sales rep in the US.
- Estimated audience: 800–1,200 reps on LinkedIn in those target companies
- Cost to reach all of them 3x per month: **$1,500–$2,500/month**
- Ad format: short video testimonial from a beta user + "Get your post-call debrief in 60 seconds" → click to the /debrief lead magnet (already built)
- Every email captured from /debrief = a warm lead Jeff didn't have to cold-approach

This is not a spray-and-pray campaign. This is a rifle pointed at 1,000 specific people in a single industry. That's the only scenario where $2K/month in LinkedIn ads actually works.

**What this buys Jeff back:** 10–15 hours per month he puts into product instead of cold outreach. That's the deal.

## The Exit Map

**Who buys us and why:**

**PatientNow / Nextech / Symplast (practice software incumbents):** They serve practices. We serve the reps who call on practices. The combined product is the full picture of the aesthetic practice relationship. Any of them with 5K practice customers would pay 3–5x revenue for the rep-side layer.

**Veeva:** Currently mid-migration, not serving aesthetics. If we get 500 reps and 3 brand contracts, we're the aesthetic beachhead they want to buy rather than build. Their 2024 revenue was $2.4B. A $20M–$40M acquisition is rounding error for them.

**PE roll-up:** A sponsor buying a portfolio of vertical SaaS companies (there are 15–20 active in MedTech/aesthetics right now) would love a profitable ARR company at 4–6x revenue in a growing vertical. At $2M ARR: $8M–$12M exit. At $3M ARR: $12M–$18M.

**Allergan / AbbVie:** Long shot, but they've acquired tools to help their field force before. If we have 200 Allergan reps using us independently (bottom-up adoption), that's a conversation about an enterprise license that turns into an acquisition discussion.

**Target exit timeline:** 24 months from now. $2M–$3M ARR. $12M–$30M exit range depending on buyer and market.

**What makes us acquirable vs. interesting:** Brand contracts (not just individual reps), a named aesthetic brand as reference customer, and retention data showing reps who use StreetNotes stay on their pipeline longer. That's it.
