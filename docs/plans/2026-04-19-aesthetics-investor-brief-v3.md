## Why This Market, Why Now

The US medical aesthetics market is at $8.4B growing to $31B by 2035. Fourteen percent CAGR. Nine thousand MedSpa locations becoming twelve thousand by 2027. Ninety percent of them independently owned — no GPO, no committee approval, no enterprise procurement cycle. The rep who knows Dr. Chen's injector preferences and shows up prepared wins the account. The one who's winging it from memory doesn't.

At the same time, Veeva — the closest thing to a rep CRM in life sciences — is mid-migration off Salesforce infrastructure. They've never treated aesthetics as a core segment. Their reps hate the tool. That migration is a 12–18 month distraction that creates a window for something better.

And AI transcription just hit the price point where this product is profitable at $199/seat. Six months ago, the economics didn't work. Now they do.

Three tailwinds, one open market. The timing isn't manufactured — it's real.

## The Compounding Math

This is where it gets interesting.

Aesthetic sales reps earn $150K–$300K. At $199/month, StreetNotes costs less than a single practice dinner. It's a non-conversation for a rep who closes deals.

But the real math isn't in individual reps. It's in brand-level deals.

Revance has 200 US reps. At $179/seat, that's $35,800 per month from one conversation with their VP of Sales. Merz has 300. InMode has 200. Evolus has 120. Three brand deals at average rep count — **$1.3M ARR before a single individual rep pays their first invoice.**

That's the number that triggers an acquisition conversation.

A Veeva, a PatientNow, a Nextech, or a PE roll-up buying vertical SaaS companies doesn't need to see 5,000 reps. They need to see 500 reps, 3 brand contracts, and a retention curve that holds. That's a 24-month build, not a 7-year one.

Every dollar we spend in the next 90 days compounds toward that exit price. Early spend in a concentrated market doesn't dilute — it entrenches. The reps who adopt now create referral loops. The brand deal we close in month 6 becomes the reference case that closes the next one in month 8. The competitor intel captured from 500 reps becomes a dataset no acquirer can replicate by building from scratch.

This is the compounding effect that makes early GTM spend so asymmetric. Spend $30K now and it's worth $300K in exit multiple in 24 months if it buys us the right customers and the right brand names on the reference list.
