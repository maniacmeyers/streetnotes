## The Short Version

We're narrowing our go-to-market to one specific segment: **the sales reps who call on aesthetic practices**. Not the practices. The reps.

You spent years at Symplast watching those reps walk through the door. You know exactly what happens when they leave — they sit in their car, maybe fire off a voice text, maybe open their phone's note app, probably forget half of what just happened. No structure. No CRM entry. No competitive intel captured. Nothing.

StreetNotes is the tool they should have been using. And nobody's built it for them.

## WHAT

**StreetNotes.ai as the Field Intelligence Platform for aesthetic sales reps.**

A rep finishes a visit with a MedSpa. Drives to the next one. Hits record on their phone for 60 seconds. StreetNotes transcribes it, extracts the structured data (contact info, deal stage, competitive mentions, follow-up tasks, product preferences at the injector level), and pushes it directly to their CRM. Done before they arrive at the next account.

The rep-facing layer of the aesthetic industry has never had a purpose-built tool. We're building it.

**Who uses it:**
- Injectable reps (Revance, Evolus, Merz, Galderma, Allergan) — 2,000+ US reps
- Device reps (InMode, BTL, Cutera, Sciton, Candela) — 1,500+ US reps
- Professional skincare reps (SkinMedica, SkinCeuticals, ZO, Alastin) — 500+ reps
- Implant reps (Mentor, Natrelle, Motiva, Sientra) — 500+ reps

**Total addressable rep population: ~3,000–6,000 US aesthetic sales reps.**

## WHY

### The Gap Is Real — and You've Seen It Firsthand

Every "CRM for aesthetics" tool you've encountered at Symplast served the *practice*. PatientNow. Nextech. AestheticRecord. Boulevard. They manage patients, scheduling, and billing for the practice owner.

Not one of them serves the rep standing in the lobby.

The closest thing on the market is Veeva CRM — built for pharmaceutical detailing, not aesthetics. Aesthetic reps are selling cash-pay cosmetic products to independent practice owners. Veeva's workflow assumes prescription tracking, Sunshine Act compliance, and 6-step physician approval chains. It doesn't know what Botox is. It doesn't track injector-level preferences. And Veeva is currently in the middle of a disruptive migration off Salesforce infrastructure — the window to displace them is 18 months wide.

The rest of the market is generic: Salesforce (not built for aesthetics), RepMove (basic voice-to-note, no intelligence), Spotio and Badger Maps (territory mapping only). Nothing voice-first, mobile-native, or built with aesthetic vocabulary.

### The Market Trajectory

- **US medical aesthetics:** $8.39B in 2025 → $31.10B by 2035. 14% CAGR.
- **US MedSpa locations:** ~9,000 today → 12,000 by 2027. 90% independently owned.
- **Procedure volume:** 15.8 million injectable procedures in the US in 2023 alone.

That growth is being driven by the reps StreetNotes serves. More locations, more launches, more competition between brands, more pressure to document, more calls per day, less time to type.

### The Workflow Fit Is Almost Eerie

Aesthetic reps drive 60% of their day. They already use voice-text constantly. They see each account 2x per quarter — so what they captured last visit is critical prep for the next. They host dinners and ride-alongs that generate complex, relationship-heavy notes. They track multiple injectors inside a single practice (each with different product preferences). They live inside a brand war — Allergan vs. Galderma vs. Merz vs. Revance — where competitive intel is literally their edge.

Every one of those realities is a direct design requirement that StreetNotes already meets.

### The Rep Economics Make Pricing Trivial

Aesthetic reps earn $150K–$300K total compensation. At $199/month per seat, the tool costs less than a single dinner event with a physician. It's a non-conversation.

### The Community Is Concentrated

One organization — **AmSpa (American Med Spa Association)** — has 9,000+ members and an annual conference (AmSpa Medical Spa Show) that puts the entire industry in one building. Every injectable brand, every device company, every practice software vendor, and thousands of practice owners and reps attend. There is no other B2B vertical where you can reach your entire market in 72 hours.

That's not a distribution advantage — that's a superpower.

### The Competitive Intel Angle Is Unique

The biggest CI companies in the world — Klue and Crayon — charge $16,000–$100,000 per year and pull their data by scraping the web. Their data is stale. Their data doesn't know what a Merz rep heard during a lunch visit at a top MedSpa in Miami.

StreetNotes captures real-time competitive intelligence from the field, rep by rep, note by note. At 1/10th the price. That's the product layer that turns a voice transcription tool into a **Field Intelligence Platform** — and it's the layer that no incumbent can replicate without rethinking their entire business model.

## HOW — GTM on $10,000–$15,000

The aesthetics industry is one of the few markets where a $15K budget beats a $150K budget if you spend it right. Here's the difference: 90% of the industry's decision-makers can be reached through warm relationships and one annual conference. Paid ads and sponsorships are how you waste money in a 5,000-person market.

### The Unfair Advantage We Haven't Used Yet: Your Network

You ran VP-level operations at Symplast. You've met hundreds of aesthetic brand managers, sales directors, and VPs at the companies that employ these reps. One warm intro from you to a VP of Sales at Revance or Merz is worth 50 cold emails from Jeff.

The first 10 customers should come from your Rolodex. That costs $0.

### Month 1 — Founder-Led Outreach ($2,000–$3,000)

**Warm network activation (Michael):**
- Identify 15–20 contacts at aesthetic brands: VPs of Sales, Regional Directors, Sales Ops leads
- Warm intro email + 30-min call: "I'm building something your reps need. Let me show you."
- Target: 3–5 pilot conversations with brand-level buyers (team deployments, not individual reps)

**Cold outreach (Jeff — $0 tools cost):**
- Build a list of 300 individual aesthetic reps on LinkedIn (Revance, Evolus, Merz, mid-tier device brands — challenger brands first, they're more motivated to adopt)
- Personalized LinkedIn DM + email: specific to their brand, their territory pressure, their workflow
- 8–12% reply rate on this audience → 24–36 conversations from 300 outreach attempts

**Product prep ($500):**
- Rewrite the lead magnet (Post-Call Debrief tool at /debrief) for aesthetic reps specifically — aesthetic vocabulary, injectable/device context, competitor brand names baked in
- One LinkedIn post announcing it to the AmSpa community
- Measure response

### Month 2 — Aesthetic Next Conference or AmSpa Event ($4,000–$5,000)

**Aesthetic Next (Dallas, September 2026) or the next AmSpa state event:**
- Attend as a founder — not as a sponsor
- Pre-book 15 coffee chats with reps and brand managers before the event via LinkedIn
- Host a small rep-only dinner or after-hours gathering (30 people, $2K–$3K): "Aesthetic Field Sales Confidential" — Jeff as MC, 2 rep panelists
- Goal: 50 conversations, 10 warm demos, 3–5 new customers from the conference alone

**This is how companies like Linear got their first customers** — presence without a booth, intimacy over scale.

### Month 3 — Concierge Onboarding + Referral Activation ($2,000–$3,000)

**Every new customer gets a 30-minute setup call:**
- Configure their CRM mapping, walk through the aesthetic vocabulary, demonstrate pre-visit brief
- At the end: "Who else on your team needs this? I'll set them up in 15 minutes."
- After 5–7 days: "You saved [X hours] this week. Know another rep who'd benefit? $500 credit if they become a paying customer."

**Superhuman used this exact model to get to their first 5,000 customers with almost no paid acquisition.** High-touch, high-retention, referral-compounding.
