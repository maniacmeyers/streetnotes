# Vertical Analysis: Aesthetics / Plastic Surgery Sales

**Purpose:** Evaluate aesthetics/plastic surgery device and injectable sales as the beachhead vertical for StreetNotes.ai. Alternative to the surgical/orthopedic device recommendation in the main moat plan.

**Date:** April 2026
**Bottom line:** **Aesthetics is a stronger beachhead than surgical/orthopedic device sales for a 2-founder team.** Smaller TAM but dramatically better fit across every operational dimension. Recommend pivoting the beachhead to this vertical and using the surgical/implant space as a year-3 expansion play.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Aesthetics/plastic surgery sales reps are a nearly perfect fit for StreetNotes across every dimension that matters for a 2-founder beachhead:

- **Market size:** ~5,000 US reps across injectables, devices, implants, and skincare, calling on ~12,000 MedSpa + plastic surgery practices (90% independently owned — no enterprise procurement friction).
- **Rep comp:** $150K–$300K total comp → $200–$500/seat pricing is trivial.
- **Workflow:** Reps drive 60% of the day between 1–2 practice visits, already use voice text heavily, and see each account only 2x per quarter (so preparation before visits is critical).
- **Competitive intensity:** Extreme. Every practice plays Allergan vs. Galderma vs. Merz vs. Revance vs. Evolus and Cutera vs. Candela vs. InMode vs. BTL vs. Sciton. CI is life-or-death, which makes the Ideabrowser "Field Intelligence Platform" wedge click perfectly.
- **Existing tools:** The gap is gaping. Practice-facing tools (PatientNow, Nextech, Symplast, AestheticRecord, Boulevard) all serve the *practice*. Veeva serves pharma (not aesthetics). **No rep-facing voice-first tool exists for aesthetic sales reps.**
- **Distribution:** One trade show (AmSpa Medical Spa Show, ~2,000 attendees, 200+ brands) reaches the entire industry. Community density is extreme.
- **Buyer mechanics:** Independent practice owners decide fast. No GPO/committee/IDN procurement. Sales cycles are days to weeks for consumables, months for capital equipment.

Compared to surgical/orthopedic device sales (TAM 10x larger but with hospital procurement friction, Stryker/Zimmer/Medtronic dominance, and heavier compliance burden), aesthetics is the harder-to-reach market that happens to be easier to serve.

### Pricing & ARR Math

- At **$199/rep/month** ($2,388/year): 5,000 reps × 100% penetration = **$12M ARR ceiling**
- Realistic 3-year capture (30% of engaged segment): **$3.6M ARR**

The vertical can support a $3M–$8M ARR business in 3 years with disciplined focus. That's a real Series A or an acquisition target at $50M–$150M valuation — not venture-scale on its own, but a credible bridge to adjacent verticals.

## 3. The Rep Workflow — Why This Is a Nearly Perfect Fit

This is where the vertical goes from "good" to "obvious." Actual quotes from industry reps ([Evolve Your Success podcast](https://evolveyoursuccess.com/aesthetic-medical-sales-with-the-lauren-brown-medical-sales-influencer/), [Aesthetic Recruiter](https://theaestheticrecruiter.net/how-to-break-into-the-aesthetics-industry/), [Medical Sales Rep Certification](https://medicalsalesrepcertification.com/a-day-in-a-life-of-a-medical-sales-rep/)):

### Every workflow signal maps to StreetNotes

| Rep reality | What StreetNotes does |
|---|---|
| **"Drives 60% of the day"** — territories are huge, windshield time dominates | Voice capture on the drive is the native interaction mode — reps are already voice-first |
| **"Uses voice text and voice email a lot"** — already a voice-native workflow | Zero behavior change to adopt StreetNotes |
| **"Sees each account twice per quarter"** — memory between visits is critical | User memory + entity graph = instant moat. "Dr. Chen's practice manager is Sarah, she bought Botox last visit, mentioned frustration with Dysport pricing." |
| **"Being prepared before being in front of somebody is critical"** — pre-visit brief is the magic moment | Pre-visit brief: here's what the rep said last time, deal history, competitor mentions, next steps outstanding |
| **"Does 2 practice visits per day"** — lots of short encounters generate lots of notes | Volume of usage = fast data flywheel |
| **"Weekend calls and texts"** — industry operates on practice hours (Saturdays are huge for aesthetics) | PWA on mobile, works offline, zero friction |
| **"Brand-heavy competitive dynamic"** — Allergan rep vs. Galderma rep vs. Merz rep fight for same chair time | CI extraction is *the* product. Klue/Crayon ($20K+/yr) fail here — they're web-scraped, not rep-captured |
| **"Hosting dinners, ride-alongs, training staff"** — relationship and event-driven sales | Voice note after every event, automatic follow-up generation, contact list enrichment |

### The unique operational wedges

1. **Rep-of-two-brands reality.** Many reps cover multiple products within one portfolio (Botox + Juvederm + SkinMedica + CoolSculpting all at Allergan). A rep needs cross-product memory on one practice: "Dr. Chen is a heavy Botox user, tried Juvederm Voluma last year, declined CoolSculpting quote." No CRM does this cleanly per rep.

2. **Dinner / event economics.** Aesthetic reps host surgeon dinners 2–4x per month. Typical 10-person dinner = $3K–$10K spend. They need to capture who attended, what was discussed, which products got interest. Voice note after dinner is ideal.

3. **Practice tier shifting.** A practice that's a B-tier account for Allergan might be A-tier for Merz (because the surgeon experimented with Xeomin). Reps track this tier state constantly. StreetNotes could formalize it.

4. **Injector-level tracking inside an account.** A single medspa might have 4–8 injectors (MD, NP, PA, RN). Each has preferences. Each sees different patients. Each is a sub-relationship. Notes at the injector level (not practice level) is huge value.

## 4. Existing Tools — The Gap

### Practice-facing (not competition; adjacent)
Every single "aesthetic CRM" result points to practice management tools. These serve the *practice owner* managing *their patients*. They do not serve the *rep selling to the practice*.

- **PatientNow** — practice management + patient marketing
- **Nextech** — EHR + practice management for dermatology/plastics
- **Symplast** — plastic surgery practice management
- **AestheticRecord** — EMR for med spas
- **Boulevard** — med spa scheduling + payments
- **Zenoti** — spa management
- **Aesthetix CRM** — plastic surgery patient conversion
- **Pabau / AestheticsPro** — aesthetic clinic EMR

None of these serves the manufacturer's sales rep calling on the practice.

### Rep-facing (the actual competition)

- **Veeva CRM (Vault CRM)** — The 800-lb gorilla in life sciences rep tools. But it's built for pharma detailing, not aesthetics. Pharma reps deal with prescription tracking, Sunshine Act reporting, HIPAA-bound physician engagement. Aesthetic reps sell cosmetic products through cash-pay practice owners. Workflow mismatch is real. Veeva is also [moving off Salesforce infrastructure to Vault Cloud by September 2025](https://www.pharmavoice.com/news/salesforce-veeva-crm-life-sciences-split-saga-clock-ticking/803290/) — migration chaos creates a window.
- **Salesforce generic** — What most mid-tier aesthetic brands use (Revance, Evolus, smaller device companies). Configurable but doesn't know aesthetics.
- **RepMove** — generic medical rep mobile CRM. Basic voice-to-note. Thin competitor but in the space.
- **Spotio / Badger Maps** — territory mapping, not voice or intelligence.

### The actual gap

**No voice-first, mobile-PWA, rep-facing tool exists specifically for aesthetic/injectable field sales.** Built with:
- Aesthetic vocabulary (neurotoxins, HA fillers, biostimulators, RF microneedling, diode lasers, etc.)
- Competitor intelligence on the specific brand matrix
- Injector-level (not just practice-level) tracking
- Event-based note capture (dinners, ride-alongs, launch events)
- Mobile-native for the 60%-driving reality

This is the whitespace. It's also protected from platform risk: Salesforce won't build this for ~5K reps, Veeva's focus is drug detailing, and the aesthetic practice software vendors have no reason to build the rep side.

**Verdict:** Aesthetics is the better beachhead by 8 of 12 meaningful dimensions. The only place surgical wins is pure TAM, and TAM at the beachhead is irrelevant — concentration and fit matter more. You can always expand from a 5K-rep beachhead into a 50K-rep adjacent market later. ServiceTitan started with plumbers before expanding to all trades. Toast started with restaurants before fighting for coffee shops.

## 6. Go-To-Market Playbook — Aesthetics Specific

### Beachhead within the beachhead

Don't try to sell all 5K aesthetic reps in year 1. Pick the narrowest slice with the highest urgency.

**Recommended initial segment: Challenger-brand injectable reps.**
- **Revance, Evolus, Merz** (not Allergan, not Galderma) — mid-tier brands hungry for any edge, less internal IT bureaucracy, CEO-accessible, less loyal to Veeva
- Target ~500–800 reps total
- Reps here are often ex-Allergan/Galderma — they've seen "good" CRM and they know current tools suck
- Challenger brands have disproportionate reason to adopt CI tooling (they're *losing* every practice to the leaders)

Secondary segment after month 6: device reps (InMode, BTL, Cutera, Sciton) — capital equipment sales, longer cycles, higher ACV per deal.

### The three go-to-market plays

**1. Own AmSpa Medical Spa Show (April 9–12, 2026 — next event in Vegas).**
- 2,000+ attendees, 200+ brands, entire industry in one hotel
- Don't exhibit — too crowded. Instead:
  - Host a rep-only after-hours event ("Aesthetic Field Sales Confidential") — 30 people, Jeff MC, dinner, panel with 2–3 top reps
  - Sponsor a small session on "Post-Call Intelligence: What Top Reps Know That You Don't"
  - Jeff attends every night's dinner, meets 30 reps, gets 30 user interviews in 3 days
  - Cost: $5K–$15K all in. ROI: entire industry knows the name in 72 hours.
- Aesthetic Next (Dallas, September 2026) = second touchpoint

**2. Jeff's content engine, aesthetics-tuned.**
- Weekly newsletter: "The Aesthetic Rep Playbook" — tactical content for reps in this vertical
- Instagram presence: aesthetic industry is Instagram-native; Jeff's LinkedIn brand doesn't translate. Hire an aesthetic-industry content partner or use a "hosted founder" model (rep-turned-influencer as co-host)
- Podcast: interview a different aesthetic rep every week. Each guest becomes a prospect + a distribution node
- YouTube: "Driving Territory" content filmed from a car (authentic to rep reality)

**3. Partner with AmSpa directly.**
- AmSpa has 9K+ members. Sponsor their newsletter, their state-level education events, their legal/compliance content
- "AmSpa Recommended" partner status = instant credibility
- Explore a co-branded content series: "AmSpa x StreetNotes — Field Intelligence"

### Product adaptations for aesthetics vertical

**Immediate (first 60 days):**

## 7. Risks & Counter-Arguments

### Against this vertical

1. **TAM ceiling is real.** 5K reps × $2,400/year = $12M ceiling. That's a small-cap business, not a venture-scale company. Counter: aesthetics is a *beachhead*, not the ceiling. Adjacent expansion to enterprise SaaS AE sellers (200K reps), surgical device reps (50K), and practice owners (12K) raises ceiling to $100M+ over 5 years.

2. **Allergan builds it internally.** They have the capital and the incentive. Counter: big-company aesthetics divisions move slowly — AbbVie's Allergan integration has taken 4+ years. Their reps hate their Salesforce instance and there's no sign of a rebuild. Also, Allergan's rep is your buyer, not your competitor — the rep will push the product UP to IT, not the other way around.

3. **Veeva pivots into aesthetics.** They've never treated it as a core segment and are in the middle of a disruptive Vault migration until late 2025. Window is 18+ months wide.

4. **Compliance creep.** If aesthetics becomes more regulated (FDA gets aggressive on off-label injectable marketing, new Sunshine-Act-like legislation), the product needs to evolve. Counter: not a deal-breaker, just a feature roadmap item.

5. **Jeff's network is SaaS, not aesthetics.** This is the most serious concern. Counter: aesthetic industry is dense and knowable — AmSpa + 200 brands + 5K reps is a community Jeff can fully penetrate in 6 months of focused networking. Probably hire a part-time aesthetics-industry advisor ($2–5K/month) to accelerate network access.

### For this vertical (strongest arguments)

1. **Product-market fit is almost obvious** — reps already drive 60% of the day, use voice text, see accounts 2x/quarter, and need prep briefs. Zero behavior change to adopt voice-first CRM.
2. **Zero dominant incumbent** — Veeva is pharma, Salesforce is generic, practice-facing tools don't compete.
3. **CI wedge monetizes immediately** — Klue/Crayon charge $16K–$100K/year. StreetNotes can capture that budget at 1/10th price with rep-sourced intel that's fresher and more relevant.
4. **Community density = capital-efficient distribution** — AmSpa + AestheticNext + 2–3 state-level events reach the entire industry for <$50K/year in sponsorships.
5. **Media amplification for free** — aesthetics is Instagram and TikTok native. User-generated content (rep testimonials on IG) doubles as marketing.

- **Month 18 (cross-user flywheel, second brand vertical added):** 7.5/10 ← **target exceeded**

## 9. What Would Kill This Recommendation

Replace the beachhead with surgical/orthopedic device if:

1. Jeff has deeper existing relationships in surgical device than aesthetics (I don't think this is true, but Jeff should confirm)
2. The 5K aesthetic rep TAM feels too small even as a beachhead (understandable but solvable via expansion)
3. Jeff viscerally cannot sell to or live inside the aesthetics culture (Instagram, influencer-adjacent, spa marketing) for 12–18 months

If none of those three hold: aesthetics is the beachhead.
